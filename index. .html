    Framework context
    Four plausible futures the business needs to be ready for
    The dashboard surfaces the signals. The room reads them against this 2x2. No position is calculated — by design.
    Slower adaptation  ←    Speed of industry adaptation    →  Faster adaptation
    Contained  ←     Severity of disruption     →  Prolonged & severe
    01
    Entrenched shock
    Sustained disruption meets a system unable to adapt. Supply chains stay fragmented, costs elevated, confidence erodes.
    02
    Coordinated resilience
    Hard times accelerate coordinated change. Industries reshore, investment accelerates, winners emerge from first movers.
    03
    Fragile stability
    Pressure eases, but vulnerabilities remain. Businesses revert to pre-crisis behaviour. The next shock finds the same weaknesses.
    04
    Rapid rebalance
    Disruption is contained. The window is used to reset structurally. Competitive lines redraw as bold movers reposition.

    About this prototype. Built to test the design of the lab dashboard. All data shown is illustrative, calibrated against current market conditions (June 2026) but not drawn from live feeds. In production, signals would be pulled from named indices: ICE Brent, ICE TTF, S&P Platts JKM, WTO Strait of Hormuz Trade Tracker, Caldara-Iacoviello Geopolitical Risk Index, Drewry World Container Index, ASX 200 VIX, NAB Monthly Business Survey, AEMO Quarterly Energy Dynamics, ABS Capex Intentions Survey, RBA. A consumer-goods tile is deliberately not included; no clean single-index signal exists for this sector and the team should decide whether to add a proxy or leave the gap honest.
